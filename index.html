From Me, Guide to practice Hiragana

Learning Hiragana is the first step to unlocking the Japanese language! Here are some helpful tips to help you master them quickly and have fun!

1. Understand the "A-I-U-E-O" Order

Japanese follows a very consistent vowel pattern: A, I, U, E, O. Once you know this, every other row follows the same sound (Ka, Ki, Ku, Ke, Ko, etc.).

2. Practice Writing (Stroke Order)

Japanese characters are meant to be written in a specific order. Writing them out by hand helps your brain remember the shapes much better than just looking at them, not just for Hiragana, but for Katakana, Kanji and Hanzi in Chinese, for me too!

3. Use Mnemonics (Depends on the person, I don't like mnemonics)

Make them easier for you to remember! For example, "あ" (A) looks like a Cross with a fish. "く" (Ku) looks like a Cuckoo bird's beak.

4. Daily Flashcard Rounds

Learn around 5-10 characters per day for a consistent routine and avoid burning out. Use the Shuffle button frequently so you don't just memorise the order of the cards, but the characters themselves.

5. Listen While You Learn

Tap the "Pronunciation" button on the back of the cards. Hearing the sound while seeing the character is the best way to train your ears. You can ask me on Discord if you don't understand a thing.

God bless with your studies! You've got this!
